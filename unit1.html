1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?
GDPR summed up is - “GDPR gives data subjects, such as users of online services, a greater degree of control over the processing of their data”[5]. GDPR stands for, General Data Protection Regulation and as stated above, it’s basically a law in place that gives users more control over what data is and isn’t used. This new law was only recently updated in 2018 and before that, the current regulation was adopted in 1995, which when you think about how far the internet has come in the past 20-25 years, is astonishing that the law wasn’t updated earlier, none the less.[6] If for example, I was about to create a chess website, that lets users login and play chess against other people from all around the world, before the user can even play a game, the user has to give details to the website, to sign up. Therefore, I (or the website) must be GDPR compliant because we are handling personal and sensitive data.
